National Teaching-Aid Creative Competition

2021 · STEM · Geometry · Merit Award

During the pandemic, I became curious about the shape of the Covid-19 virus and started studying its structure. Using recycled materials, I built a model to help people visualize and understand it better—showing how creativity can make science more engaging.

Hands-On Science: Building an Icosahedron to Understand the Invisible

I built a 3D icosahedron model inspired by COVID-19 visuals, to explore why viruses often appear spherical. The piece was exhibited at the national teaching-aid competition and received a Merit Award as the youngest participant. Merit Award as one of the youngest participants.

  • Focus: Polyhedra, minimal surface, stability
  • Skills: Research, modeling, public presentation
  • Solo entry: independent presentation and Q&A
