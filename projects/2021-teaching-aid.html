National Teaching-Aid Creative Competition (2021)

During the pandemic, I became curious about the shape of the COVID-19 virus and started studying its geometry. Using recycled materials, I built a 3D icosahedron model to explore why viruses often appear spherical. The project was showcased at the National Teaching-Aid Creative Design Competition, where I received a Merit Award as one of the youngest participants.

  • Focus: Polyhedra, minimal surface, stability
  • Skills: Research, modeling, public presentation
  • Solo entry: Independent presentation and Q&A
